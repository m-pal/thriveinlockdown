MIND

"Happiness depends on your mindset and attitude."

- Roy T. Bennett

Adjusting to quarantine and the new "normal" is not an easy task. Prolonged restrictions and uncertainty, social distancing as well as soaring unemployment leave many of us worried about the future. This puts many of us in distress, impacting not only our wallets and social life but also our mental health. For that reason now it is more than ever necessary for all of us to prioritise taking care of our wellbeing. We recommend you to follow our tips to make coping easier in this difficult times.

Start from establishing new routines. Plan and structure your days to avoid aimlessly drifting and spending the whole day binge-watching Netflix. Make sure you stay active and find a replacement for your workouts that are quarantine-friendly. Try out new hobbies you didn't have time for before. Experiment with stillness and minfulness through meditation and yoga.
Do not forget to also keep in contact with others. Especially extroverts might find it more difficult to face loneliness. Staying in touch with your friends and loved ones who are experiencing similar issues can bring a sense of community.
Self-isolation might have a detrimentally influence our wellbeing. Research has shown that the negative effects range from lower mood, irritability to anxiety and even symptomps of PTSD. A few ways that you can reduce impact of isolation include keeping yourself occupied and active, staying connected with others as well as maintainig a daily rhythm. In case your symptomps get worse, do not hesitate to reach out to a therapist who will be able to help you to get back on track.
